Welcome to Perspectives of Parth

Every stone has a story. Every script holds a secret.

I’m Parth, a wanderer in the world of India’s forgotten temples, silent sculptures, and sacred texts. This space is where my journey through history, archaeology, and Indology unfolds from ancient carvings to timeless philosophies.

Here, you’ll find glimpses of field notes, reflections on temple art, unsung epigraphs, and the echoes of India’s cultural soul. Whether it’s decoding a forgotten symbol or reimagining an epic like the Mahabharata, I believe the past still speaks we just need to listen.

About Me

I’m Parth, a student of History at Fergusson College, Pune, and a passionate seeker of India’s layered past. My academic and creative journey is rooted in a deep fascination with archaeology, Indology, temple architecture, and iconography. For me, history is not just a subject; it is a lived legacy carved in stone, whispered in ancient scripts, and echoed through timeless philosophies.

My most recent research explored the Bhumij temples near Pune, a project that allowed me to combine fieldwork with the study of sculptural forms and sacred symbolism — a step further in my lifelong quest to document and interpret India’s monumental heritage.

Beyond the academic realm, I express myself through writing and performance. I am the author of Vedh, a science fiction novel that bridges imagination with deeper truths, and I’m currently working on a creative retelling of the Mahabharata, bringing fresh eyes to one of India’s greatest epics. My words have found their place not just on pages, but on stages across Maharashtra as a public speaker and poet, sharing thoughts that connect hearts with heritage.

Interests & Vision

I’m committed to unearthing stories from the past whether through temple inscriptions or the poetic rhythms of ancient texts. My work blends rigorous historical research with the power of storytelling, making history accessible, meaningful, and inspiring.
